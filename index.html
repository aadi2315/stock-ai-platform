About the Company:

Reliance Industries Limited is India's largest company by market capitalisation and revenue. Founded in 1966 by Dhirubhai Ambani, it has grown into a global conglomerate. The company operates across energy, petrochemicals, retail, digital services (Jio), and media. Reliance Retail is India's largest retailer, and Jio is India's biggest telecom provider. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, it employs over 2,36,000 people and is listed on both NSE and BSE.

Financial Literacy

Master these 6 essential terms before you begin investing.

📊
Stock
A stock represents a small unit of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner (shareholder) of that business.
e.g. Buying 1 share of Reliance makes you a tiny co-owner of Reliance Industries.
🪙
Share
A share is the individual unit of a company's stock. A company divides its total value into millions of shares which are sold publicly.
e.g. TCS has issued ~3.6 billion shares in the market.
💰
Investment
Putting your money into stocks, bonds, or assets with the goal of growing it over time. Investing involves risk but also potential reward.
e.g. Investing ₹10,000 in Infosys 10 years ago would be worth ₹80,000+ today.
📈
Profit
Profit is the gain you make when the value of your investment increases or when a company earns more than it spends.
e.g. Buy at ₹500, sell at ₹700 → Profit of ₹200 per share.
⚠️
Risk
The possibility that your investment might lose value. Higher potential returns usually come with higher risk. Always assess risk before investing.
e.g. A small startup stock has high risk but also high growth potential.
💵
Dividend
A portion of a company's profit paid out to shareholders regularly. It's a reward for holding the company's stock — like earning interest.
e.g. Reliance paid ₹9 per share as dividend in 2024.
